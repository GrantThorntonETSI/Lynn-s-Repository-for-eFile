Trademark Details

* = Required

Trademarks and service marks: Are generally words, symbols, designs, or combinations of these. Marks are often brand names; they work to identify the sources of goods or services, showing that they come from particular manufacturers or sellers. Trademarks are for goods, which are physical things that are sold to others, like shoes. Service marks are for services that are provided to others, like tax preparation services.

Certification Mark

A certification mark is a type of trademark that is used to show consumers that particular goods and/or services, or their providers, have met certain standards. (See TMEP Section 1306Opens in new window)

Collective Mark

A collective mark is mark used by members of a collective organization to identify their goods and/or services and distinguish them from the goods and/or services of non-members. (See TMEP Section 1303Opens in new window)

Collective Membership Mark

A collective membership mark is a mark used by the members of a particular organization to signify that they are members of that organization (e.g., a social club or labor union). (See TMEP Section 1304Opens in new window)
